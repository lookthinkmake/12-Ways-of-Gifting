The Thinkery is an evolution of the Austin Children’s Museum founded on the mission to create innovative learning experiences that equip and inspire the next generation of creative problem solvers. Austin Pets Alive!

Austin Pets Alive! is a nonprofit organization dedicated to helping keep Austin no-kill. Their innovative programs save more than 6,000 animals from euthanasia annually, and since 2008, APA! has saved more than 25,000 dogs and cats from being killed at Central Texas shelters.

Project Transitions

Project Transitions is dedicated to serving people with HIV and AIDS by providing hospice, housing and support in a compassionate and caring environment. Project Transitions operates three transitional housing programs for people living with HIV and AIDS, helps residents apply for permanent housing and achieve their personal goals of independent lives, and they provide an exhaustive list of wrap-around social services to provide support to people living with HIV and AIDS.

Front Steps

Based on the belief that all people deserve the dignity of a safe place to call home, Front Steps has provided a pathway home through shelter, affordable housing, and community education since its founding in 1997. Through managing the Austin Resource Center for the Homeless, Front Steps provides resources to over 600 homeless men and women daily, as well as emergency overnight shelter to 230 men each night.

Austin Diaper Bank

Austin Diaper Bank is dedicated to providing diapers to Austin families in need, offering them a clean, reliable supply of diapers for a healthy and comfortable quality of life. Since June 2013, the Austin Diaper Bank has partnered with over 30 partner groups and social service agencies to distribute more than 100,000 diapers across the Austin area.

United Way for Greater Austin

United Way for Greater Austin (UWATX) has a dual mission; first to build philanthropy and second to make measurable changes in the lives of the working poor. For almost 90 years, they’ve run employee giving campaigns and built public/private partnerships to address the greatest need in the Austin community and currently run Austin’s largest volunteer network. In addition, each year United Way of Greater Austin collects toys and holiday items for families in need throughout our community. UWATX helps Austin thrive by helping the working poor overcome barriers to economic opportunity through supportive programs, grants, and community services.

Any Baby Can

Any Baby Can is a nonprofit in Austin, Texas that serves pregnant women, young children and families through home visitation programs, community classes and support groups. Based on the foundation that every child deserves a strong family, Any Baby Can focuses on supporting the family through parenting education and child abuse prevention, and family health by serving children with chronic illnesses and developmental delays.

Growing Roots is an Austin-based nonprofit organization that connects families of children with special needs to the resources they need to navigate the complicated health and education systems. The mission of Austin Zoo & Animal Sanctuary is to assist animals in need through rescue, rehabilitation and education. SafePlace

SafePlace is an Austin-based agency that works to end sexual and domestic violence through safety, healing, prevention and social change. SafePlace not only provides confidential services and support to survivors of past or recent trauma related to sexual and domestic violence, but also provides extensive community outreach, education and prevention programs through the Expect Respect Program for teens and youth, Disability Services, Deaf Services, Community Education and Community Dialogue. With their innovative programs, SafePlace touches the lives of more than 30,000 women, children, and men in our community each year.

The Capital Area Food Bank of Texas

The Capital Area Food Bank of Texas is the largest hunger-relief charity in Central Texas, providing food and grocery products, nutrition education, and social services outreach to 300,000 clients each year through a network of 300 Partner Agencies in twenty-one Central Texas counties. In the past year, the Food Bank has provided roughly 24.5 million meals to a service area that covers almost 20,000 square miles in Central Texas.

Austin Partners in Education

Austin Partners in Education is a nonprofit organization that connects the community and the classroom to promote academic excellence and personal success for all students in AISD. Focusing on Austin’s ISD Title I schools in under-resourced communities and serving predominately economically disadvantaged students, APIE delivers innovative, volunteer-driven academic and supportive mentoring programs.
